# Learning Objectives - Humanoid Robot Architecture

This section outlines the specific learning objectives for the Humanoid Robot Architecture chapter.

## Primary Objectives

1. **Understand Humanoid Design Principles**: Comprehend the fundamental principles that guide the design of humanoid robots, including anthropomorphic considerations and functional requirements.

2. **Identify Major Subsystems**: Recognize the key subsystems of a humanoid robot including locomotion, manipulation, perception, and control systems.

3. **Analyze Stability Challenges**: Understand the challenges in maintaining balance and stability in humanoid robots during static and dynamic activities.

4. **Evaluate Trade-offs**: Assess the trade-offs between different architectural approaches in terms of complexity, efficiency, and functionality.

5. **Recognize Safety Considerations**: Identify the safety requirements and considerations specific to humanoid robot systems.

## Secondary Objectives

1. **Historical Development**: Understand the evolution of humanoid robot architecture and key milestones in the field.

2. **Component Integration**: Grasp how different components and subsystems integrate to form a cohesive humanoid system.

3. **Control Strategies**: Recognize different control strategies used in humanoid robots for movement and interaction.
