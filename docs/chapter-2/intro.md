## What Sensors, Actuators, and Control Systems Are

In the context of Physical AI and robotics, three fundamental components work together to create intelligent behavior:

- **Sensors**: Devices that perceive and measure aspects of the environment or the robot's internal state
- **Actuators**: Mechanisms that enable the robot to exert forces and move in the physical world
- **Control Systems**: Computational frameworks that process sensor data and command actuators to achieve desired behaviors

Together, these components form the bridge between the digital intelligence of AI algorithms and the physical reality of robotic systems.

## Why They Are Critical for Physical AI

Sensors, actuators, and control systems are the foundation of Physical AI because they determine how effectively a robot can interact with its environment. Without proper sensing, a robot cannot understand its surroundings. Without capable actuators, it cannot affect change in the world. Without robust control systems, it cannot coordinate its actions effectively.

These components directly impact:
- A robot's ability to navigate and manipulate objects
- Its safety when interacting with humans and environments
- Its adaptability to changing conditions
- Its energy efficiency and performance
