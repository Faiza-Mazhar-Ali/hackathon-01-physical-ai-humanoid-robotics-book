# Learning Objectives - Sensors and Actuators

This section outlines the specific learning objectives for the Sensors and Actuators chapter.

## Primary Objectives

1. **Identify Sensor Types**: Recognize the major types of sensors used in robotics including vision, tactile, proprioceptive, and environmental sensors.

2. **Understand Actuator Principles**: Comprehend the principles of operation for different types of actuators including electric motors, pneumatic, and hydraulic systems.

3. **Analyze Integration Challenges**: Understand the challenges in integrating multiple sensors and actuators into a cohesive system.

4. **Evaluate Performance Trade-offs**: Assess the trade-offs between different sensor and actuator technologies in terms of accuracy, speed, power consumption, and cost.

5. **Apply Selection Criteria**: Apply appropriate selection criteria for sensors and actuators based on specific robotic application requirements.

## Secondary Objectives

1. **Calibration Techniques**: Understand the importance of sensor calibration and common calibration techniques.

2. **Control Strategies**: Recognize different control strategies for actuator systems.

3. **Sensor Fusion**: Understand the concept of sensor fusion and its importance in robotics.

4. **Safety Considerations**: Identify safety requirements and considerations for sensor and actuator systems.
